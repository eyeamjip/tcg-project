## Our three-phase approach

Construction cleaning is rarely one pass. We work in phases so the right level of cleaning happens at the right point in your project.

### 1. Rough clean
After framing and rough trades. Large debris removal, broom-and-haul on construction waste, surface dust knockdown so finishers can work in a cleaner environment.

### 2. Final clean
After all trades are out. The detailed pass — every surface, every fixture. Sticker and adhesive removal from windows and appliances, cabinet interiors, light fixtures, vents, baseboards, hardware polished, floors deep-cleaned.

### 3. Touch-up clean
Right before walkthrough or handover. Final dust pass, fingerprint and footprint cleanup, glass redone, anything the day-of inspection would catch.

You can book one phase or all three. Most contractors book the final clean and the touch-up as a package.

## FAQ

### How much does post construction cleaning cost in Las Vegas?
Most post-construction cleaning in Las Vegas prices between $0.20 and $0.40 per square foot for a final clean. A 2,500 sq ft new build typically runs $500–$1,000 depending on condition. Rough cleans are quoted lower; commercial or restaurant final cleans can run higher. Get a free quote with photos or a walkthrough.

### Do you clean during construction or only at the end?
Both. We phase: rough clean during construction, final clean before handover, touch-up clean right before walkthrough. Book one or all three.

### Are you insured for commercial construction sites?
Yes — fully licensed, bonded, and insured. We carry general liability and can provide a certificate of insurance to general contractors and property owners on request.

### How fast can you turn around a final clean?
For most homes and units, we can complete a final clean in one day with a two- or three-person crew. Larger commercial buildouts take 2–3 days. We give you a real timeline when we quote.

### Do you remove construction debris and haul it away?
We handle surface debris and bag it for haul. Large construction waste — pallets, drywall sheets, lumber — is a contractor or dumpster trade, not a cleaning trade. We can recommend local haulers if you need one.
